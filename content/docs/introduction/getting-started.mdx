## Quick Start: Students

### Step 1: Create Your Account

<Cards>
  <Card title="Visit Homepage">
    Navigate to the SkillBridge homepage and click Sign Up in the top right corner.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Choose Account Type">
    Select Student Account to access student-specific features and project discovery tools.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Complete Registration">
    Enter your email, create a secure password (minimum 8 characters), or use social login options.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Verify Email">
    Check your inbox and verify your email address to activate your account and begin using the platform.
  </Card>
</Cards>

<Callout title="Professional Email" type="success">
Use a professional email address that you check regularly to ensure you don't miss important notifications about applications and messages.
</Callout>

### Step 2: Build Your Profile

A complete profile significantly increases your chances of being selected for projects.

**Essential Information**

<Cards>
  <Card title="Profile Photo">
    Upload a professional headshot or clear photo that represents you professionally.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Professional Bio">
    Write a brief introduction (2-3 sentences) highlighting your interests and goals.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Contact Information">
    Provide details on how organizations can reach you for project opportunities.
  </Card>
</Cards>

**Education Details**

- School or university name
- Degree or major program
- Expected or actual graduation date
- Relevant coursework (optional but recommended)

**Skills & Experience**

- Technical skills with proficiency levels
- Soft skills (communication, teamwork, problem-solving)
- Work experience, internships, or volunteer work
- Portfolio links (GitHub, LinkedIn, personal website)

### Step 3: Explore Projects

Navigate to the Browse page to discover opportunities that match your skills.

<Cards>
  <Card title="Use Search">
    Enter keywords related to your interests, skills, or desired industry to find relevant projects.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Apply Filters">
    Select categories, required skills, timeline, and commitment level to narrow results.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Read Descriptions">
    Carefully review project requirements, deliverables, and expectations before applying.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Check Timelines">
    Ensure you can commit to the project duration and meet all deadlines.
  </Card>
</Cards>

### Step 4: Apply to Your First Project

When you find a project that matches your skills and interests:

<Cards>
  <Card title="Review Project Details">
    Click on the project card to view the complete description, requirements, and timeline.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Assess Fit">
    Evaluate whether your skills match the requirements and you can commit to the timeline.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Complete Application">
    Click Apply and fill out the form explaining your interest, experience, and approach.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Track Status">
    Monitor your application status through your dashboard and respond promptly to messages.
  </Card>
</Cards>

**Application Best Practices**

- Explain why you're interested in this specific project
- Highlight relevant experience and skills
- Outline your approach to completing the project
- Clearly state your availability and timeline
- Customize each application—avoid generic submissions

<Callout title="Application Success" type="success">
Customized applications that demonstrate understanding of the project requirements are significantly more likely to be accepted than generic submissions.
</Callout>

### Step 5: Start Your Journey

Once accepted to a project:

**Communication**

Use the integrated chat system to discuss project details, clarify requirements, and ask questions.

**Set Expectations**

Ensure you clearly understand all deliverables, deadlines, and evaluation criteria before beginning work.

**Plan Milestones**

Break the project into manageable tasks with checkpoints to track progress and stay organized.

**Maintain Updates**

Keep the organization informed of your progress with regular status updates and early notification of any challenges.

**Complete Successfully**

Finish strong, submit all deliverables on time, and earn your verified completion badge.

## Quick Start: Businesses & Organizations

### Step 1: Create Organization Account

<Cards>
  <Card title="Visit Homepage">
    Navigate to SkillBridge and click Sign Up in the top right corner of the page.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Select Business Account">
    Choose Business Account to access organization features and project posting tools.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Provide Details">
    Enter organization name, your email address, and create a secure password.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Verify Email">
    Confirm your email address to activate your account and begin setting up your profile.
  </Card>
</Cards>

### Step 2: Complete Organization Profile

Make your organization attractive to qualified students by providing comprehensive information.

**Basic Information**

<Cards>
  <Card title="Organization Identity">
    Provide official name, industry classification, and organization size.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Location Details">
    Specify city, state/region, and country for geographic context.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Visual Branding">
    Upload professional logo and organization imagery to build recognition.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Contact Information">
    Designate primary contact person for student communication.
  </Card>
</Cards>

**Detailed Profile**

- Mission statement and organizational values (3-4 sentences)
- Official website URL for additional information
- Social media profiles (optional but recommended)
- Brief history or background of the organization

### Step 3: Post Your First Project

Create a compelling project posting that attracts qualified students.

**Project Basics**

- Clear, descriptive project title
- Appropriate category selection
- Realistic timeline with start date and duration
- Expected commitment level (hours per week)

**Project Details**

- Comprehensive project description (3-4 paragraphs)
- Specific deliverables and expected outcomes
- Required technical and soft skills
- Preferred qualifications or nice-to-have skills
- Stipend information (if applicable)

**Clear Expectations**

- Well-defined project scope and boundaries
- Communication preferences and frequency
- Submission requirements and formats
- Evaluation criteria for project completion

<Callout title="Project Success" type="success">
Well-defined projects with clear expectations and realistic timelines attract better qualified candidates and lead to more successful outcomes.
</Callout>

### Step 4: Review Applications

When students apply to your project, use these tools to evaluate candidates effectively.

<Cards>
  <Card title="View Applications">
    Access all applications from your organization dashboard with filtering and sorting options.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Review Profiles">
    Examine student qualifications, portfolios, previous projects, and skill assessments.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Compare Candidates">
    Use side-by-side comparison tools to evaluate multiple applicants simultaneously.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Communicate">
    Reach out via chat to ask clarifying questions or request additional information.
  </Card>
</Cards>

### Step 5: Collaborate & Complete

Working effectively with students to achieve successful project outcomes.

**Onboarding**

Provide necessary resources, access credentials, and background information to set students up for success.

**Set Milestones**

Define clear checkpoints for progress reviews and interim deliverable submissions.

**Maintain Communication**

Schedule regular check-ins to review progress, address questions, and provide guidance.

**Provide Feedback**

Offer constructive feedback throughout the project to help students improve and succeed.

**Verify Completion**

Review final deliverables, endorse the student's work, and provide completion verification.

## Best Practices

<Tabs items={['For Students', 'For Organizations']}>
  <Tab value="For Students">
### Student Best Practices

**Do:** Complete your profile thoroughly before applying to projects. Customize each application for the specific project. Communicate professionally and promptly at all times. Meet all deadlines and honor your commitments. Ask questions when requirements are unclear. Update organizations on your progress regularly.

**Don't:** Apply to projects you cannot realistically commit to. Send generic, copy-paste applications. Ghost organizations after being accepted to a project. Take on more projects than you can handle. Wait until the last minute to ask critical questions.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab value="For Organizations">
### Organization Best Practices

**Do:** Provide clear, detailed project descriptions with realistic scope. Respond promptly to applications and student questions. Offer constructive feedback throughout the project. Recognize excellent work with endorsements and verification. Maintain professional, supportive communication. Set realistic timelines and expectations.

**Don't:** Post vague project requirements or undefined outcomes. Change project scope significantly after student acceptance. Expect professional-level work without guidance and support. Leave applications unanswered for extended periods. Provide only negative feedback without actionable guidance.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
