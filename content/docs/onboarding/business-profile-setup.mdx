Your organization profile serves as the foundation of your SkillBridge presence. A complete, professional profile attracts qualified students, builds trust, and sets the stage for successful project collaborations.

## Why Your Profile Matters

### Building Trust and Credibility

Students carefully evaluate organizations before applying to projects. Your profile serves as their first impression and primary information source.

**Impact of Profile Quality:**
- Complete profiles receive approximately 4x more applications than incomplete ones
- Professional branding significantly increases the quality of applications received
- Clear mission statements help attract students whose values align with yours
- Detailed information reduces pre-application questions and streamlines the process

### Profile Benefits

A well-crafted organization profile delivers multiple advantages:

**Visibility**: Higher placement in student searches and browse results
**Quality**: More qualified applications from students with relevant skills
**Engagement**: Better communication and relationship building with candidates
**Credibility**: Enhanced reputation and trustworthiness on the platform
**Pipeline**: Stronger ongoing talent acquisition for current and future projects

## Logo and Branding

### Logo Technical Requirements

**File Format**: PNG with transparent background preferred, or high-quality JPG
**Minimum Dimensions**: 400x400 pixels
**Maximum File Size**: 5MB
**Aspect Ratio**: Square (1:1) recommended for best display across platform
**Resolution**: High resolution ensuring clarity at various sizes

### Logo Best Practices

**Professional Quality**: Use your official company logo with clear, crisp rendering

**Visual Clarity**: Ensure good contrast against both light and dark backgrounds for accessibility

**Simplicity**: Keep design recognizable and readable when scaled to smaller sizes

**Consistency**: Match branding used across other platforms (website, social media, marketing materials)

**Authenticity**: Use current, official logos rather than outdated or unofficial versions

### Uploading Your Logo

1. Click Edit Profile in your organization dashboard
2. Navigate to the Branding section
3. Click Upload Logo button
4. Select your prepared logo file
5. Crop to square aspect ratio if needed
6. Save changes to update your profile

### Cover Image (Optional Enhancement)

Add a cover photo to create visual impact:

**Dimensions**: 1200x400 pixels recommended
**Content**: Office environment, team photos, product imagery, or brand visuals
**Quality**: High resolution, professional photography or design
**Relevance**: Images that represent your organization's culture and work


## About Us Section

### Crafting Your Organization Description

The About Us section is your organization's story and value proposition. This is often the most-read part of your profile, making it critical for attracting quality applicants.

**Recommended Length**: 400-600 words (3-5 paragraphs)

**Effective Structure:**

1. **Introduction**: What your organization does and your core business
2. **Mission and Impact**: Why you exist, your goals, and the change you create
3. **Unique Differentiators**: What sets you apart from similar organizations
4. **Student Value Proposition**: What students gain by working with you
5. **Invitation to Engage**: Encouraging students to explore opportunities

### Writing Guidelines

**Create Authentic Content**: Tell your genuine story rather than generic corporate messaging

**Be Specific with Impact**: Include relevant achievements, metrics, and concrete outcomes

**Explain Student Value**: Clearly articulate what students will learn and gain

**Use Professional Tone**: Maintain professionalism while remaining approachable and authentic

**Highlight Culture**: Share what makes your organization a great place to work and learn

**Avoid Common Pitfalls**:
- Excessive jargon or corporate buzzwords that obscure meaning
- Unsupported claims without evidence or examples
- Focusing solely on organizational needs without addressing student benefits
- Dense, unreadable paragraphs without clear structure
- Ignoring what actually matters to student contributors
- Generic descriptions that could apply to any organization in your sector

## Contact Information

### Primary Contact Details

Provide reliable, monitored contact information that students can use for inquiries and communication.

**Required Fields:**
- **Primary Email**: General inquiries email that is checked regularly
- **Contact Person**: Full name of primary point of contact for student inquiries
- **Job Title**: Official title of the contact person

**Optional but Recommended:**
- **Phone Number**: Direct line for urgent communications
- **Office Address**: Physical location if students may visit or work on-site
- **Contact Hours**: When the contact person is available for communication

### Communication Best Practices

**Responsiveness**: Aim to respond to student inquiries within 24-48 hours maximum

**Direct Access**: Provide a specific person's contact when possible rather than generic info addresses

**Current Information**: Update contact details immediately when staff changes occur or roles shift

**Professionalism**: Ensure the contact person understands their role representing the organization

<Callout title="Contact Visibility" type="warn">
This contact information is visible to students who apply to your projects or express interest in your organization. Ensure it leads to someone prepared to engage with student inquiries professionally.
</Callout>

## Mission and Values

### Defining Your Mission Statement

Your mission statement clearly communicates your organization's purpose and helps students understand what drives your work.

**Effective Mission Components:**
- What your organization does (core activities and services)
- Who you serve (target beneficiaries or customers)
- What impact you create (outcomes and change you produce)

**Example Mission Statements:**

"Our mission is to provide affordable, accessible mental health resources to underserved communities through technology platforms and community partnerships."

"We exist to accelerate clean energy adoption by making solar installation simple, affordable, and accessible for residential homeowners across the Pacific Northwest."

### Core Values Definition

List 3-5 core values that guide your organizational culture and decision-making:

**Technology Company Example:**
- **Innovation**: We embrace new ideas and experimental approaches to problem-solving
- **Integrity**: We operate with transparency and honesty in all client relationships
- **Collaboration**: We believe diverse perspectives create better solutions
- **Impact**: We measure success by positive outcomes for our users and communities
- **Growth**: We invest in continuous learning and professional development

**Nonprofit Organization Example:**
- **Equity**: We prioritize access and opportunity for historically marginalized communities
- **Empowerment**: We build capacity rather than creating dependency
- **Sustainability**: We design programs for long-term community benefit
- **Partnership**: We work collaboratively with community stakeholders
- **Accountability**: We measure and report our impact transparently

### Why Mission and Values Matter

Students increasingly seek organizations whose values align with their own personal values and career goals. Clear mission and values help:

- Attract students who will be culturally compatible and motivated
- Set appropriate expectations for collaboration style and organizational culture
- Build stronger, more meaningful working relationships
- Create more successful project outcomes through shared purpose

**Active Accounts Only**: Include only social profiles you actively maintain and update regularly

**Public Accessibility**: Ensure all linked profiles are publicly viewable without login requirements

Organizations with dormant or rarely-updated social media accounts should not include those links, as they can create negative impressions about organizational viability.

## Profile Optimization Strategies

### Using Rich Content Effectively

**Visual Elements**: Add images of your team, office environment, or completed projects
**Social Proof**: Include testimonials from past student contributors
**Project Showcase**: Highlight successful completed collaborations
**Success Stories**: Share specific outcomes and impacts from student work

### Being Specific and Concrete

**Avoid vague descriptions**: "We're a tech company looking for talented students"
**Use specific details**: "We're a B2B SaaS company seeking students skilled in React and Node.js to help build our customer analytics dashboard for financial services clients"

Specificity helps qualified students self-select and increases application quality.

### Showcasing Organizational Culture

Provide insight into your work environment:
- Describe your collaboration style and communication norms
- Highlight team dynamics and mentorship approach
- Share what makes your organization unique as a place to work
- Mention any learning opportunities, perks, or benefits available to student contributors

### Keywords and Search Optimization

Help students discover your organization:

**Industry Terms**: Use standard terminology for your sector
**Technology Stack**: Mention specific tools, frameworks, and platforms
**Geographic Location**: Include city and region for local opportunities
**Organization Type**: Clearly specify nonprofit, startup, enterprise, etc.

### Maintaining Current Information

Regular profile maintenance ensures accuracy and relevance:
- Update team size as your organization grows or changes
- Refresh the About Us section annually or when mission evolves
- Add new achievements, awards, and recognition promptly
- Update contact information immediately when staff changes occur
- Showcase recent projects and accomplishments

## Privacy and Visibility Settings

### Student-Visible Information

**Always Public (visible to all students):**
- Organization name and logo
- Industry category and size
- General location (city and state)
- All posted projects and their descriptions
- Public student reviews and ratings

**Application-Only Information (visible only to applicants):**
- Full contact details including email and phone
- Detailed internal organizational information
- Private notes or additional context
- Specific project application requirements

### Managing Your Organizational Presence

Control your profile visibility and engagement level:

**Active Profile**: Fully visible in searches, actively accepting applications, regularly posting new projects
**Limited Profile**: Only students you directly contact can view full organizational details
**Inactive Profile**: Not accepting new projects or applications, profile not appearing in searches

Adjust these settings based on your current capacity to engage with students and manage projects.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Practices That Damage Your Profile

**Incomplete Information**: Leaving required sections blank significantly reduces trust and application rates

**Generic Descriptions**: Copy-paste content that could describe any organization in your industry

**Vague Opportunities**: Failing to clearly explain what students will actually do and learn

**Unrealistic Expectations**: Expecting professional-level deliverables without providing adequate support or mentorship

**Poor Communication**: Slow responses to inquiries or unclear, inconsistent information

**Outdated Content**: Old contact details, defunct social media links, or information about past projects no longer relevant

### Best Practices to Follow

**Complete Everything**: Even optional sections add substantial value and credibility

**Be Authentic**: Share your real organizational story, mission, and culture

**Specific Projects**: Create clear, well-defined opportunities appropriate for student skill levels

**Realistic Scope**: Design projects that students can meaningfully contribute to with guidance

**Prompt Communication**: Reply to student inquiries within 24-48 hours consistently

**Regular Updates**: Keep all profile information current and accurate through active maintenance
