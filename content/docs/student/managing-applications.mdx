## Responding to Organizations

### When Organizations Reach Out

**Common Requests:**
- Clarifying questions
- Additional portfolio examples
- Interview scheduling
- Availability confirmation

**Response Best Practices:**
- Reply within 24 hours
- Answer completely and clearly
- Maintain professional tone
- Ask own questions if needed

### Interview Preparation

**If Invited to Interview:**

**Prepare:**
- Review project description thoroughly
- Research organization
- Prepare questions
- Test technology (for video calls)
- Have portfolio examples ready

**Common Interview Questions:**
- Why are you interested in this project?
- Walk me through your relevant experience
- How would you approach this project?
- What's your availability like?
- Do you have questions for us?

**Your Questions to Ask:**
- What does success look like for this project?
- What support and resources will be available?
- What's the review and feedback process?
- Are there opportunities for future projects?

## Handling Rejection

### Professional Response

**If Not Selected:**

**Good Response:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for considering my application and for the update. While 
I'm disappointed, I appreciate you taking the time to review my 
materials.

I remain very interested in [Organization Name]'s mission. If similar 
opportunities arise in the future, I'd welcome the chance to apply 
again.

Best wishes with your project!

[Your Name]
```

**Why This Works:**
- Professional and gracious
- Leaves door open
- Maintains relationship
- Shows maturity

### Learning from Rejection

**Productive Steps:**
- Request feedback (optional)
- Review your application
- Identify improvement areas
- Update profile if needed
- Apply lessons to next application

**Questions to Ask:**
- Was there anything specific that could have strengthened my application?
- What skills or experience would you recommend developing?
- Would you be open to my applying to future projects?

## Withdrawing Applications

### When to Withdraw

**Valid Reasons:**
- Accepted another project
- Schedule changed
- No longer available
- Skills mismatch discovered
- Personal circumstances

### How to Withdraw Professionally

**Process:**
1. Navigate to application
2. Click "Withdraw Application"
3. Select reason
4. Add optional message
5. Confirm withdrawal

**Message Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

I need to withdraw my application for [Project Name]. I've accepted 
another project that overlaps with this timeline, and I don't want 
to overcommit.

Thank you for your consideration, and I hope to apply for future 
opportunities with [Organization].

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Managing Multiple Projects

### Balancing Workload

**If Accepted to Multiple Projects:**

**Assess Capacity:**
- Total hours committed per week
- Project timeline overlaps
- Other obligations (school, work)
- Buffer for unexpected

**Making Decisions:**
- Prioritize based on:
  - Learning value
  - Portfolio impact
  - Organization fit
  - Compensation
  - Timeline flexibility

**Communicating:**
- Be honest about capacity
- Don't overcommit
- Decline professionally if needed
- Suggest alternatives if possible

### Time Management

**Weekly Planning:**
- Block time for each project
- Schedule regular check-ins
- Set personal deadlines
- Leave buffer time

**Staying Organized:**
- Use project management tools
- Keep communication separate
- Track deliverables and deadlines
- Maintain professional boundaries
